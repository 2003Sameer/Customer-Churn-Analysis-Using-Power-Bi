# Customer-Churn-Analysis
This project focuses on analyzing customer churn behavior in a telecom company using data cleaning, transformation, and interactive visualization in Power BI.

## Project Description

Customer churn is one of the biggest challenges for telecom companies. Losing customers directly affects revenue and increases the cost of acquiring new customers.

This project focuses on analyzing customer churn behavior in a telecom company using data cleaning, transformation, and interactive visualization in Power BI. The objective is to identify patterns and key factors responsible for customer churn and provide actionable business insights to reduce churn and improve customer retention.

The dashboard helps stakeholders quickly understand why customers are leaving, which segments are most affected, and what strategies can help retain customers.


## Project Overview
### What is Customer Churn?

-> Customer churn refers to the percentage of customers who stop using a company's product or service during a specific time period.
For telecom companies, churn happens when customers:
Cancel their subscriptions
Switch to another telecom provider
Stop using the service completely
High churn leads to Loss of revenue , Increased marketing costs , Reduced customer lifetime value


### What is Churn Rate?

-> Churn rate is the percentage of customers who leave the company within a specific period.
#### Formula:
Churn Rate = (Number of Customers Lost / Total Customers) × 100

#### Example:
If a telecom company has 1000 customers and 270 customers leave, the churn rate becomes:
Churn Rate = 27%
Reducing churn rate is a critical business goal for telecom companies.

### Key Insights 

1️⃣ Customers with Month-to-Month Contracts Have Higher Churn

Customers who subscribe on short-term contracts churn more frequently compared to long-term contract users.

2️⃣ High Monthly Charges Increase Churn Risk

Customers paying higher monthly charges tend to leave more frequently, possibly due to better offers from competitors.

3️⃣ Customers Without Tech Support Are More Likely to Churn

Users without technical support services show higher churn rates, indicating support services improve retention.

4️⃣ Fiber Optic Internet Users Have Higher Churn

Customers using fiber optic internet services churn more than DSL users, possibly due to service dissatisfaction or pricing.

5️⃣ New Customers Are More Likely to Leave

Customers with low tenure (new customers) are more likely to churn.

### Business Recommendations to Reduce Churn

1️⃣ Encourage Long-Term Contracts

2️⃣ Improve Customer Support Services

3️⃣ Optimize Pricing Plans

4️⃣ Focus on New Customer Retention

5️⃣ Bundle Additional Services
